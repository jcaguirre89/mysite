My name is Cristobal Aguirre. I studied Economics in Chile, and moved to Vancouver with my wife in mid 2017 after 5 months travelling around South East Asia. I set up this website as a personal project to improve my web app development skills with the Django Web Framework.
As a self-taught programmer, I owe a great debt to the open source community, and the people who took the time to write the tutorials and answers I used to learn. I will use this site as a platform to give back to the community as best I can, helping others who run into the same roadblocks I have in the past. And because the best way to get an answer on the internet is not by posting a question but an incorrect answer, I also hope more experienced programmers will chip in and share their experience for everyones' benefit.
My fondest memories while studying economics are the times I would open a new dataset in STATA or MATLAB, looking to uncover relationships and prove/refute my priors. I became a convert after being introduced to Python a few years ago, drawn by it's breadth, simplicity and a massive online community. This blog will double as a platform for me to explore and learn new Data Science techniques, trying to complement them with an economics flavour.
